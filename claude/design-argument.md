## The Person

This site is designed for my cousin, who is about to graduate high school and attend Yale University in the fall. I chose her for this project because she is entering a major life transition: moving from Atlanta to New Haven and adjusting from high school to college out of state.

## The Problem

For a recent high school graduate, moving to another state can feel overwhelming. There are many separate things to figure out at once, including what to pack, what dorm supplies are needed, what should be stored or shipped, and how to prepare for a new living environment. Because these tasks are often scattered across notes, shopping lists, family conversations, and online searches, the process can quickly become stressful.

## The Definition of "Helped"

The goal of this project is to create a tool that feels genuinely useful during this transition. The Move Book acts as a one-stop planning space that helps my cousin organize her move in a clearer and more manageable way. By guiding her through packing decisions and storage or shipping options, the site aims to reduce stress and give her more confidence as she prepares for college.

**Observable signal of success:** *"She finishes a packing decision list without abandoning it halfway."*

This is the standard every feature is evaluated against. A feature that makes the list longer, more confusing, or easier to walk away from is wrong, regardless of how good it looks.
